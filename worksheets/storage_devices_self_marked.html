Understanding Storage Devices: Storage devices are used to save data and programs for use now or in the future. The three main storage types you need to know are magnetic, optical, and solid-state storage. Each type has unique characteristics, advantages, and disadvantages that make them suitable for different tasks.
  • Magnetic storage (like hard drives) - high capacity and low cost per GB
  • Optical storage (like CDs and DVDs) - often used for media distribution
  • Solid-state storage (like SSDs and USB flash drives) - fast, reliable, no moving parts
